Legal Requirement

Why CNG Hydro Testing is Mandatory

Under the Gas Cylinder Rules, 2004 and PESO guidelines, every CNG vehicle cylinder must undergo hydrostatic testing every 3 years — no exceptions.

⚖️

Gas Cylinder Rules, 2004 — Mandatory Testing

As per Indian Standards IS 8451:2000 and the Gas Cylinder Rules, 2004, all CNG on-board cylinders must be periodically hydro-tested every 3 years. Authorized CNG refuelling stations are required to refuse filling cylinders whose test certificate has expired. Non-compliance may also affect your vehicle's insurance coverage. Only PESO-certified centres like ours can issue valid test certificates accepted pan-India.

🔬

Detect Hidden Damage

CNG cylinders operate at 200–250 bar pressure. Micro-cracks, corrosion, and metal fatigue are invisible to the naked eye but detectable through hydrostatic testing before they cause failures.

⛽

CNG Stations Won't Fill Expired Cylinders

Filling stations are mandated by PESO to check test validity. An expired certificate means your vehicle cannot be refuelled until the cylinder is retested and recertified.

🛡️

Legal & Insurance Compliance

An untested cylinder can void your vehicle insurance policy in the event of an accident. The test certificate is a legal document required for continued road use.

🚗

Better Vehicle Performance

Testing includes internal cleaning to remove oil build-up and moisture, which improves gas flow efficiency and can enhance your engine's performance after the test.

Step by Step

Our Testing Process

A thorough 12-step procedure performed by trained technicians

1

De-pressuring & De-gassing

Safe release of all residual CNG gas in a controlled environment.

2

Valve Removal

Cylinder valve removed; threads and sealing areas inspected for damage.

3

Internal & External Cleaning

Thorough cleaning to remove oil, rust, and impurities from inside and outside.

4

Visual Inspection

Check for dents, corrosion, burns, scratches, and structural anomalies.

5

Wall Thickness Measurement

Ultrasonic thickness testing to verify metal integrity without cutting.

6

Hydrostatic Test (Water Jacket)

Cylinder pressurized beyond operating pressure; volumetric expansion measured precisely.

7

Drying & Painting

Cylinder thoroughly dried and freshly painted to prevent future corrosion.

8

Nitrogen Purging & Valve Refitting

Cylinder purged with nitrogen; valve refitted with fresh seals.
